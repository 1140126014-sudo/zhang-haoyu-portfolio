# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This portfolio is for design collaborators, hiring/recruiting viewers, creative directors, brand teams, and clients who need to quickly understand Zhang Haoyu's visual judgment, AIGC production ability, and ability to turn ideas into presentable work.

Visitors arrive in a scanning mindset. They need a strong first impression, credible project evidence, clear contact paths, and enough structure to understand which projects are finished cases, expandable archives, or reserved slots for future material.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Zhang Haoyu as an AIGC visual designer working across visual identity, brand systems, AI video, character/IP design, product prototypes, and archive-style case studies.

Success means the first viewport clearly communicates the person and direction, the project grid feels like a curated visual archive rather than a template, and the Toy IP case remains a strong proof-of-work moment without hiding contact and experience information.

## Brand Personality

Technological, cinematic, precise.

The visual voice should feel like a dark exhibition interface with iOS-like system clarity: quiet glass, thin lines, crisp typography, confident spacing, and a single high-impact red signal.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like a generic developer portfolio, a startup SaaS landing page, a purple AI-gradient template, or a decorative card grid.

Avoid repeated small uppercase section labels, fake product screenshots, overused glassmorphism, ornamental stripes, excessive shadows, decorative section numbers, and placeholder project cards that look unfinished.

## Design Principles

1. Show proof before claims. Use real portraits, project images, and archive systems wherever possible.
2. Keep the system precise. Typography, lines, and spacing should feel engineered, not decorative.
3. Let red behave like a signal. Use the brand red for decisive hierarchy, not as a blanket wash.
4. Separate finished work from reserved archives. Unfinished material can still look intentional.
5. Preserve interaction quality. Toy IP tabs, responsive layouts, reduced motion, and contact links must stay reliable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve semantic navigation, tab/tabpanel structure, visible focus states, reduced-motion behavior, lazy image loading, and responsive layouts across desktop, tablet, and mobile.
